Welcome To Education in India

India, being the land of diverse cultures, a birthplace of some of the world’s greatest religions and history offers a wide range of options for international students. The thought of studying in at one of the most exciting countries can seem challenging at first but often opens up new perspective and experience of life.

Highlights

Education in India

The Indian education system is currently mainly comprised of primary education, secondary education, senior secondary education and higher education. Depending on what you study, attaining your undergraduate degree in India can take three to five years. Postgraduate courses are generally two to three years long. After completing a postgraduate degree, there is the option of continuing on with doctoral studies and research at various educational institutes.

History of India

India is a land of ancient civilization. India's social, economic, and cultural configurations are the products of a long process of regional expansion. Indian history begins with the birth of the Indus Valley Civilization and the coming of the Aryans. These two phases are usually described as the pre-Vedic and Vedic age. The history of India is one punctuated by constant integration with migrating peoples and with the diverse cultures that surround India.

Lifestyle in India

India is the birthplace of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism, and other religions.India is one of the most religiously and ethnically diverse nations in the world, with some of the most deeply religious societies and cultures. Virtually all regions of India have their distinctive places of pilgrimage, local saints and folk heroes, religious festivals, and associated fairs. Some of the widely celebrated festivals in India are Holi, Diwali, Eid and Christmas.
